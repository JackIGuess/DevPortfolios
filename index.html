— Your Options —

Three ways to get a portfolio online.

Each approach works for different goals. Here is an honest comparison so you can choose what fits your situation.

  • DIY Website Builders

    Wix, Carrd, Squarespace, and similar tools

    A solid choice if you enjoy learning web design and want full control over updates yourself.

    • Low monthly cost, but subscriptions add up over time
    • Time spent learning layout, embeds, and mobile formatting
    • Generic templates not built around gameplay or design documents
    • Easy to end up with a site that looks fine but reads like every other student

    Best for: students who want to learn web design alongside their portfolio.

  • Generic Portfolio Services

    Freelancers and agencies outside games

    A practical option if the lowest upfront price is the priority and your work is straightforward to present.

    • Often template-based with limited games-specific structure
    • May not understand gameplay reels, GDD pages, or team project credits
    • Less context for level design, combat, AI systems, or group workflows
    • Communication can feel transactional rather than peer-to-peer

    Best for: people who mainly need a clean page and already know exactly how to frame their work.

  • Working With Me

    Student-focused games portfolios

    Built for students and graduates who want professional presentation without agency pricing - from someone who was in your position recently.

    • Custom-coded HTML/CSS portfolios you fully own
    • One-off payment with no recurring builder fees
    • Direct communication via Discord and email
    • Layouts shaped around how game studios skim applications
    • Guidance from a recent graduate who has shipped industry work

    Best for: game dev students and graduates preparing for placements, internships, or junior roles.

— FAQ —

Got questions? Straight answers.

Common questions from students before they get started.

Who is this service for?

Game development students - design, programming, art, or production - preparing for placements, graduate schemes, or junior roles. If you have projects to show but no confident way to present them online, this is for you.

What do I need to provide?

At minimum: project titles, short descriptions, and gameplay video links or files. For Standard and Premium, add screenshots, GDD pages, and any case study notes you already have. I will tell you exactly what is missing after you enquire.

Do I get a say in how it looks?

Yes. I handle layout, navigation, and how your work is presented - the parts that are hard to get right without web experience. You are not handed a random design: we agree on styling (colours, fonts, tone) early, and revision rounds are there to adjust the look until it feels like yours.

How long does a portfolio take?

Starter typically takes 5-7 days, Standard 7-10 days, and Premium 10-14 days - assuming your content is ready. Delays usually come from waiting on assets, not the build itself.

Can you help write case studies?

Premium includes structure guidance and light editing to help your projects read clearly to recruiters. Starter and Standard expect you to provide the written content; I format and place it for maximum impact. Full ghostwriting is available as a separate add-on if needed.

Do I keep the files after launch?

Yes. Every tier includes full source handover at launch - HTML, CSS, and assets - so you can host, edit, and update the site yourself. You own what we build together.

Why not use Wix or Squarespace?

Those tools are great if you want to learn web design yourself. They become harder when you need gameplay embeds, design document slideshows, and a layout that reads like a games portfolio rather than a generic CV site. My service is for students who would rather focus on their projects and applications than spend evenings fighting a page builder.

Can I update my portfolio later?

Yes. You receive the full source files, so you can edit them yourself anytime. If you would rather not, book a Portfolio Refresh to add new projects, update your experience, or prepare for a new application round.

Do I need a maintenance subscription?

No. Static portfolios hosted on GitHub Pages do not need ongoing maintenance fees or monthly builder subscriptions. Your site sits online until you choose to update it. When you are ready for changes, a one-off refresh or add-on project is all you need.
